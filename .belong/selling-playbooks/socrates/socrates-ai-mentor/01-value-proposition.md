## Playbook Rules

- Present the Service as `Socrates, an AI mentor`: an always-on AI mentor that helps every employee make better use of approved AI tools.
- Qualify buyers who are traditional companies, especially mid-market companies and SMBs, trying to improve practical AI adoption across employees.
- Treat employees as the primary end-user persona; treat company leaders, managers, HR/L&D, operations, IT, and AI enablement owners as likely economic buyers or approvers.
- Prioritize buyers who have one or more of these pains:
  - Employees are unsure which AI tool to use for specific tasks.
  - Teams have approved AI tools but adoption is inconsistent.
  - Managers need confidence that employees are using AI safely and within policy.
  - Employees need lightweight coaching rather than a one-time course.
- Describe the core outcomes as:
  - Stack-aware AI tool recommendations.
  - Everyday Q&A for safe, practical AI use.
  - Personalized coaching and learning paths.
  - Manager visibility into AI fluency and progress.
- Use these Service Tags for marketplace search:
  - `ai-adoption`
  - `employee-enablement`
  - `ai-mentor`
  - `tool-guidance`
  - `workforce-training`
  - `ai-governance`
  - `slack`
  - `teams`
  - `browser`
  - `privacy`
- Pursue use cases such as:
  - Company-wide AI enablement.
  - Department-level AI rollout.
  - AI tool selection and safe-use guidance.
  - Employee onboarding into approved AI tools.
  - Ongoing prompt and workflow coaching.
  - Team fluency measurement.
- Use the Alcaparros success case when a buyer needs evidence that Socrates can support organization-wide transformation. Describe it as a K-12 school in the Bogotá, Colombia area that used Socrates to help teams become AI-first. Do not invent metrics, quotes, dates, or detailed claims beyond the approved proof point.
- Avoid or escalate use cases where the buyer wants:
  - A generic chatbot with no connection to company tools or policies.
  - A one-off AI course with no ongoing support.
  - Unapproved use of sensitive data.
  - Guarantees of employee performance improvement that are not backed by agreed measurements.
  - Custom enterprise integrations beyond the confirmed Socrates delivery scope.
  - Illegal activity, policy evasion, unauthorized data access, or guidance that would knowingly violate law or buyer policy.
- Ask seller-led discovery questions:
  - Which employee groups or departments need Socrates first?
  - Which AI tools are currently approved, piloted, or prohibited?
  - What AI usage policies should Socrates follow?
  - Where should employees access Socrates: Slack, Teams, browser, email, or another channel?
  - What questions are employees asking today about AI tools, prompts, privacy, or workflows?
  - Who approves recommended toolkits or policy-sensitive guidance?
  - What would make this rollout successful after 30, 60, and 90 days?
- Minimum qualification criteria:
  - Buyer has a defined team or employee group to onboard.
  - Buyer can provide or confirm approved AI tools and AI usage policies.
  - Buyer has a manager, owner, or approver for toolkit recommendations.
  - Buyer accepts a workflow where employee guidance happens in their existing work surfaces.
  - Buyer accepts that Socrates will not support illegal activity or intentional policy evasion.

## Source Notes

Derived from approval chat:
- Socrates is one single purchasable Service.
- The Service name is `Socrates, an AI mentor`.
- Socrates is an always-on AI mentor for company teams and employees.
- The Service helps employees choose the right AI tool, ask everyday questions, and learn practical AI workflows.
- The buyer pain is AI tool sprawl, uncertainty about which tools are safe or useful, and uneven employee fluency.
- Socrates works where employees already work, such as Slack, Microsoft Teams, browser, and email.
- The employee journey includes joining a workspace, completing a short interview, receiving a tailored AI stack, and getting ongoing coaching.
- The manager or responsible owner can review and approve proposed toolkits.
- Privacy and policy fit are part of the value proposition: Socrates recommends tools grounded in the company's approved stack and policies.
- Primary end-user personas are employees of traditional companies, especially mid-market companies and SMBs.
- Socrates is available worldwide with no current geography, language, implementation capacity, or onboarding lead-time bottleneck.
- Socrates should avoid illegal activity. Beyond that, no sectors, data types, or tool stacks are currently excluded.
- Human-provided proof point: Socrates helped Colegio Hacienda Los Alcaparros, a K-12 school in the Bogotá, Colombia area, drive a deep transformation so its teams could become AI-first.
