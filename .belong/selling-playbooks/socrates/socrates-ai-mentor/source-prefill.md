## Extracted Facts

- Socrates positions itself as an always-on AI mentor for teams.
- The core buyer problem is team confusion around AI tool choice, safety, prompting, and practical adoption.
- The Service helps employees pick the right AI tool, answer everyday questions, and learn through coaching.
- Socrates is meant to live where employees already work, including Slack, Microsoft Teams, browser, and email.
- Socrates says it can know an organization's approved tools and policies.
- The site emphasizes confidentiality, tenant-contained conversations, no training on customer data, and audit trails for compliance teams.
- The onboarding motion includes a short employee interview, a personalized AI toolkit recommendation, manager approval, then ongoing coaching.
- Public availability signal: onboarding in Q2 2026 with limited spots.
- The Service should be named `Socrates, an AI mentor`.
- Primary end-user personas are employees of traditional companies, especially mid-market companies and SMBs.
- Geographic and language availability is worldwide.
- There are no known implementation capacity or onboarding lead-time bottlenecks.
- Sector and tool-stack fit is broad, except Socrates should not support illegal activity.
- Human-provided proof point: Socrates has a strong success case with Colegio Hacienda Los Alcaparros in the Bogotá, Colombia area, helping a K-12 school team make a deep AI-first transformation.
- External verification: Colegio Hacienda Los Alcaparros is a school in La Calera/Cundinamarca near Bogotá, founded in 1992, serving preschool through high school / primary and secondary education.
