# Negotiations

[Back to index](index.md) | Previous: [Legal And Contracts](03-legal-and-contracts.md) | Next: [Way Of Work During An Active Service](05-active-service-work.md)

This section defines how far the Selling Agent can negotiate without human approval.

## Source Signals

Look for sales approval matrices, discount policies, proposal history, deal desk notes, lost-deal notes, procurement redlines, pricing exceptions, and leadership guidance.

## Required Fields

- Discount limits
- Scope concession limits
- Timeline concession limits
- Payment concession limits
- Contract fallback positions
- Buyer qualification rules
- Walk-away conditions
- Cumulative exposure checks
- Competitive engagement posture
- What must be escalated before counteroffer, acceptance, or signature
- Standing Authorization for autonomous negotiation
- Disclosure scope: what to reveal to a buyer versus keep internal (state a clear decision such as "no discount available" without exposing limits, margins, authority thresholds, capacity, or policy)

## Quality Bar

The section is `Done` when the Selling Agent can negotiate price, scope, timeline, payment, and contract terms inside a clear authority envelope and knows when to stop.

## Guardrails

- Do not let the Selling Agent trade legal risk for revenue without approval.
- Do not discount below the approved floor.
- Do not expand scope while keeping price and timeline unchanged unless explicitly allowed.
- Do not reveal discount limits, margins, authority thresholds, capacity, or internal policy to a buyer; give a clear decision and keep the reasoning internal.
