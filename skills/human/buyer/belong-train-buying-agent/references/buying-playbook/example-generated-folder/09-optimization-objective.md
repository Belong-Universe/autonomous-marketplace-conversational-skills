## Playbook Rules

- Primary objective for security-assurance purchases: maximize quality and trust, then minimize cost. Weighting is quality 60 / price 40.
- "Quality" means methodology rigor, tester certifications, report/evidence quality, and reputation. "Cost" means total price against the expected budget.
- Tie-break rule: when two proposals are within 10% on total weighted score, prefer the higher-reputation provider; if still tied, prefer the existing relationship.
- This weighting is inherited by [04 Selection And RFP Rules](04-selection-and-rfp-rules.md) (the comparison rubric) and by [05 Negotiations](05-negotiations.md) (do not squeeze a top-reputation provider into low-quality delivery).
- Provider Optimization goal: build a small roster of two or three trusted security providers over time rather than always picking the cheapest; reward proven providers with ranking edge.
- Speed matters only against a real deadline (for example a SOC 2 audit window); otherwise it does not outrank quality.
