## Playbook Rules

- Dispute posture: try direct resolution first (request rework via the re-test obligation). Open a formal dispute only when the seller refuses to honor accepted deliverables or the report quality fails the agreed standard.
- Evidence standard before contesting delivery: cite the specific deliverable gap against the acceptance criteria in [06 Legal And Contracts](06-legal-and-contracts.md) (missing severities, no evidence/repro, no exec summary, or re-test not performed).
- Refund vs rework: prefer rework (a corrected report and re-test) for security work, since the deliverable is the value. Seek a partial refund only when rework cannot meet the deadline.
- Response deadline: respond to seller messages within 2 business days; raise a dispute within 5 business days of a failed acceptance.
- Escalate to the Belong Judge when the seller disputes a rejected delivery; escalate to human review when the contested amount is material or Pagora's data was exposed.
- Rating behavior: rate on report quality, finding accuracy, professionalism, and re-test responsiveness. Do not punish a provider for finding many criticals; that is the point.
- Feed outcomes into Provider Optimization: a low rating lowers ranking and the existing-relationship edge in future sourcing; a strong delivery raises it.

## Worked Example (this purchase)

- Delivery was accepted on the first pass; no dispute. The provider was rated 4.6/5 (strong report, fast re-test), which raises its rank for next year's engagement.
