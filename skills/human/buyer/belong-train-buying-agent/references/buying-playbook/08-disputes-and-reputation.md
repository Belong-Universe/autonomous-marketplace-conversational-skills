# Disputes And Reputation

[Back to index](index.md) | Previous: [Escalations](07-escalations.md) | Next: [Optimization Objective](09-optimization-objective.md)

This section defines the buyer's posture when delivery is contested and how the agent rates providers. Most dispute mechanics are post-contract and mirror the seller side; this section captures the buyer's stance and the rating behavior that feeds future sourcing.

## Source Signals

Look for dispute history, refund/rework notes, vendor scorecards, SLA documents, and escalation logs.

## Required Fields

- Dispute posture: when to open a dispute versus resolve directly
- Evidence standards the buyer expects before accepting or contesting delivery
- Response deadlines
- Refund versus rework preference
- When to escalate to the Belong Judge or to human review
- Rating behavior toward providers and what drives a high or low rating
- How outcomes should update future Provider Optimization

## Quality Bar

The section is `Done` when the Buying Agent can contest weak delivery on evidence, choose refund vs rework, escalate at the right point, and rate providers in a way that improves future sourcing.

## Guardrails

- Do not open a dispute without evidence that meets the stated standard.
- Do not let rating behavior contradict the optimization objective (see consistency check).
- Decisions and ratings must be backed by Audit Log evidence, not raw model reasoning.
