# Optimization Objective

[Back to index](index.md) | Previous: [Disputes And Reputation](08-disputes-and-reputation.md) | Next: [Human-To-Human Meetings](10-human-to-human-meetings.md)

This section defines what the Buying Agent optimizes across providers and how it resolves trade-offs. It frames the weighting that [Selection And RFP Rules](04-selection-and-rfp-rules.md) and [Negotiations](05-negotiations.md) depend on, so surface it during the opening context gathering and confirm it here.

## Source Signals

Look for procurement KPIs, cost-savings targets, quality/SLA goals, strategic-supplier lists, OKRs, and category strategy notes.

## Required Fields

- What to optimize across providers: cost, quality, speed, reliability, or strategic relationships
- The key trade-offs between them and how to resolve a tie (the tie-break rule)
- Provider Optimization goals that steer ranking and repeat-buying decisions
- Any categories, segments, or deal sizes to treat differently

## Quality Bar

The section is `Done` when the Buying Agent can rank two different proposals and concede in negotiation against a clear, stated objective.

## Guardrails

- Do not let selection or negotiation tactics contradict the stated objective (for example, "maximize quality" but always pick the cheapest).
- The optimization objective is playbook guidance mapped to `--optimization-goals`; reflect it through selection, negotiation, and rating rather than as a one-off override.
