# Human-To-Human Meetings

[Back to index](index.md) | Previous: [Way Of Work During An Active Service](04-active-service-work.md) | Next: [Escalations](06-escalations.md)

This section defines when humans should meet, how the Selling Agent prepares them, and how meeting outcomes become operational state.

## Source Signals

Look for sales call notes, kickoff agendas, workshop templates, delivery meeting templates, escalation notes, calendar patterns, and follow-up emails.

## Required Fields

- Meeting types the Selling Agent may propose
- Meeting triggers
- Required attendees and roles
- Scheduling authority and constraints
- Availability source: each human's required Calendly link (Google Calendar optional)
- Rules for proposing a meeting to the buyer's agent
- Rules for accepting, counter-proposing, or declining a meeting from the buyer's agent
- How acceptance shares the Calendly link and how the proposing agent books the slot
- What the agent can confirm autonomously versus what needs human confirmation
- Agenda and preparation expectations
- Materials to send before the meeting
- Decisions to capture during or after the meeting
- Follow-up format
- Meeting-related escalations
- When meetings are optional, recommended, or required

## Quality Bar

The section is `Done` when the Selling Agent knows when to schedule a meeting, who should attend, how to prepare both sides, and how to convert outcomes into auditable follow-up.

## Guardrails

- Do not schedule humans into meetings when an async answer is enough.
- Do not leave meeting decisions outside the audit trail.
- Do not let a meeting override contract, payment, or scope rules without the required approval path.
