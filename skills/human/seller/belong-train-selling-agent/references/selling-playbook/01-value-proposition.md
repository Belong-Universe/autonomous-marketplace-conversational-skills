# Value Proposition

[Back to index](index.md) | Previous: [Source Prefill](source-prefill.md) | Next: [Monetization Models](02-monetization-models.md)

This section defines what the Service is, who it is for, why buyers buy it, and how the Selling Agent qualifies fit.

## Source Signals

Look for service pages, decks, case studies, proposals, testimonials, CRM notes, onboarding forms, discovery notes, and product docs.

## Required Fields

- Service name
- Plain-language Service description
- Primary buyer personas
- Buyer pains, desired outcomes, and trigger events
- Differentiators and proof points
- Availability, geographies, capacity, or scheduling limits
- Service Category for marketplace search
- Use cases the Selling Agent should pursue
- Use cases the Selling Agent should avoid
- Seller-led discovery questions
- Minimum qualification criteria

## Quality Bar

The section is `Done` when the Selling Agent can explain the Service clearly, match it to the right buyers, reject poor-fit demand, and ask discovery questions that reveal fit.

## Guardrails

- Do not turn vague positioning into unsupported claims.
- Do not promise outcomes the seller cannot evidence.
- Do not treat company-level capabilities as Service-specific capabilities unless the seller confirms the Service provides them.
