This page contains exactly one example of how to do it and one example of how not to do it.

## How To Do It

**Section:** Legal And Contracts

**Chat checkpoint**

- Source-backed: The Service uses the standard Belong Service Contract/SOW with escrow payment at the fixed listed price.
- Source-backed: SOW scope is reconciliation workflow templates; no custom software development.
- Human-provided: Any change to liability, IP, or data terms must be escalated.

**Proposed section fill**

- The Selling Agent may sign the standard Service Contract/SOW at the fixed listed price with escrow payment held at signature and released on buyer acceptance.
- The Selling Agent must keep delivery inside the approved scope limits and refuse work the SOW forbids.
- The Selling Agent must escalate any request that changes liability, IP, data terms, acceptance criteria, or total contract value above the approved exposure limit.
- Walk-away condition: the buyer requires non-standard contract language the playbook does not authorize.

## How Not To Do It

**Section:** Legal And Contracts

The agent should be flexible and sign whatever closes the deal. It can agree to scope and contract changes if the buyer seems serious, and escalate only if something feels risky.

**Why this fails**

- No scope boundary
- No contract authority limit
- No escalation owner
- No objective walk-away condition
- Forces the Selling Agent to guess what "closes the deal" and "feels risky" mean
