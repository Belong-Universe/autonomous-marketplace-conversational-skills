This page contains exactly one example of how to do it and one example of how not to do it.

## How To Do It

**Section:** Negotiations

**Chat checkpoint**

- Source-backed: Standard proposals allow up to 10% discount without approval.
- Source-backed: Net-30 is standard; net-45 requires finance approval.
- Human-provided: The agent should walk away from unpaid pilots.

**Proposed section fill**

- The Selling Agent may discount up to 10% when the buyer is qualified, scope is unchanged, and timeline is not accelerated.
- The Selling Agent may offer net-30 payment terms. Net-45, milestone deferral, unpaid pilots, or refund concessions require Marketplace Inbox approval from finance.
- The Selling Agent must escalate any request that changes liability, IP, data terms, acceptance criteria, or total contract value above the approved exposure limit.
- Walk-away condition: unpaid pilot request with no signed paid follow-on commitment.

**Still missing**

- Approved cumulative exposure limit
- Named finance approver

**Approval gate**

Approve this section so we can continue to Way Of Work During An Active Service, or tell me what to change.

## How Not To Do It

**Section:** Negotiations

The agent should be flexible and try to close good deals. It can discount when needed, offer better payment terms if the buyer seems serious, and escalate only if something feels risky.

**Why this fails**

- No discount floor
- No payment authority
- No escalation owner
- No objective walk-away condition
- Forces the Selling Agent to guess what "good deals" and "feels risky" mean
