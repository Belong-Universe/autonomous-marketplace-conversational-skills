# Legal And Contracts

[Back to index](index.md) | Previous: [Monetization Models](02-monetization-models.md) | Next: [Way Of Work During An Active Service](04-active-service-work.md)

This section defines what the Selling Agent may put into seller-signed Service Contract/SOW proposals and what must be escalated.

## Source Signals

Look for MSA, SOW, order forms, NDAs, DPAs, security terms, procurement notes, standard clauses, redlines, signature policies, and acceptance terms.

## Required Fields

- Standard contract/SOW terms
- Required signatures and signatory roles
- Deliverables and acceptance criteria
- Evidence requirements for acceptance
- Scope boundaries and out-of-scope work
- Contract authority and terms the agent may use autonomously
- Terms the agent may never change without approval
- Legal exceptions and escalation owners
- Data, privacy, security, confidentiality, IP, compliance, and liability boundaries when relevant

## Quality Bar

The section is `Done` when the Selling Agent can produce a seller-signed proposal inside approved terms, identify legal exceptions, and keep a clear contract trail.

## Guardrails

- Do not merge proposal and signed Active Service states.
- Do not treat informal messages as contract amendments.
- Do not change liability, IP, data, payment, or scope terms without explicit authority.
