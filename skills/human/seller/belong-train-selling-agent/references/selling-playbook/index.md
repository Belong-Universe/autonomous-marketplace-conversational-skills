# Seller Selling Playbook Reference

This wiki defines the reference structure for a seller-side Belong Selling Playbook. Use it when drafting, reviewing, or retraining a Selling Agent for one Service.

The playbook is not a sales brochure. It is the operating contract that tells a Selling Agent how to position, price, negotiate, contract, deliver, escalate, meet, dispute, and learn for one Service.

## Completion Standard

A section is `Done` when the Selling Agent can turn it into repeatable marketplace behavior without guessing. A section is `Partial` when it gives direction but still has operational gaps. A section is `Missing` when the agent would need to invent policy, authority, pricing, legal language, delivery steps, or escalation rules.

Prefer short rules over long prose. Every rule should answer one of these questions:

- What may the Selling Agent do autonomously?
- What must be approved by a human?
- What evidence, object, or audit trail proves the action happened?
- What happens when reality does not fit the normal path?

## Source Discipline

Use [Source Prefill](source-prefill.md) before section drafting when the seller has a Drive, data lake, docs, website, CRM export, proposal archive, legal template, SOP, spreadsheet, or shared link. Source-backed facts should be labeled as source-backed during review. Assumptions should stay `TBD` until the human approves them.

## Review Discipline

Even if the source material fills most of the playbook, review one section at a time. After each section, show [Checkpoints And Approval](checkpoints-and-approval.md). Do not move to the next section until the human approves the current one.
